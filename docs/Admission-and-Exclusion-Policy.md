# Admission and Exclusion Policy

The default bottleneck is review attention, not idea supply. A useful addition is admitted because it changes a downstream decision, artifact, or behavior for a named consumer—not because it is plausible, comprehensive, or easy to generate.

## Admission record

Every nontrivial skill, pack, template, recurring report, automation, or major vault section must name:

1. the observed problem and its evidence;
2. the consumer who should notice improvement;
3. the durable destination;
4. what it replaces, merges, or overlaps;
5. context, storage, execution, and human-review cost;
6. blast radius and authority boundary;
7. a verification method tied to downstream change;
8. a rollback or kill condition.

Prefer the smallest destination that can carry the outcome: chat for ephemeral work, a note for durable evidence, a template for repeated structure, a skill for repeatable judgment, and a deterministic script for stable transforms. Search and merge before creating a parallel owner.

## Exclusion and rejection

Reject, externalize, or defer an addition when it has no named consumer, duplicates an owned surface, lacks redistribution rights, turns private material into public seed content, has no bounded retrieval/maintenance case, exceeds the active context or review budget, treats measurement as action authority, or requires unsupported ownership of upstream state.

Plausible filler is not a substitute for source-derived seed content. Personal preferences do not become universal doctrine. Drift-prone recipes remain upstream pointers until real demand and compatibility evidence justify local ownership.

A rejection record states the evidence checked, the reason, the chosen disposition, and what new evidence would change it. Rejection is durable learning, not a hidden omission.

## Parked-state discipline

`watch`, `no-action`, and `defer` are decisions. Each must record:

- the state-change or return condition;
- a review or decay date;
- what happens if nothing changes;
- the owner of the next review.

At decay, renew explicitly with new evidence, demote, merge, or kill. An item does not remain parked merely because nobody reviewed it. When an unresolved queue exceeds its item or review-minute budget, pause generation and run a bounded resolve pass.

## Closed disposition vocabulary

Operational outcomes use:

```text
act | watch | no-action | no-edge | blocked | done | merge | defer | kill | needs-human
```

Source-file consolidation uses:

```text
copy | adapt | merge | supersede | external-pointer | reject
```

Labels do not replace evidence; they state the next governed state after the evidence is recorded.
