# Strategic Interaction and Goodhart Check

Trigger this lens only when actors can observe, adapt, coordinate, defect, signal, or optimize against a rule. Record `not-triggered` when those conditions are absent; do not decorate a non-strategic mechanism with game language.

For triggered cases, answer:

1. Which actors choose, and what are their incentives and constraints?
2. What information or signals can each actor observe or manipulate?
3. Is interaction one-shot or repeated, and what coordination or retaliation is possible?
4. What behavior is likely after each actor adapts—the plausible equilibrium rather than the intended first move?
5. Which metric is a proxy for the actual goal?
6. How could optimizing or reporting that proxy break its relationship to the goal (Goodhart pressure)?
7. What failure mode follows, and which countermeasure changes incentives or measurement?
8. What observation would show the equilibrium or Goodhart story is wrong?

Keep the answers domain-specific. A plausible strategic story is a hypothesis until actor behavior or source-of-record evidence tests it.
