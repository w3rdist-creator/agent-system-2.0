# Pattern — Bounded Selection Pressure

## Observations in two independently sourced domains

Research domain: [[Source Note - Source to Synthesis]] requires a preserved source, evidence and limits, a disposition, and a useful downstream output before durable promotion. It is a prescribed workflow, not measured performance.

Operations domain: [[Source Note - Loop Scoreboard]] requires a recurring loop to expose impact and burden, receive a closed verdict, and stop when unread, inert, duplicative, or too costly. It is likewise a prescribed workflow.

The domains come from different pinned public repositories and concern different generated objects: research candidates versus recurring operational outputs.

## Explicit causal mechanism

1. Generation creates candidates faster than usefulness can be inferred.
2. Durable queue or ledger state makes unresolved candidates visible.
3. Review compares each candidate's downstream change against its review cost and limitations.
4. A closed disposition retains, changes, merges, defers, or removes the candidate.
5. Repeated disposition applies selection pressure, so retained material is tied to use instead of mere production.

The transferable mechanism is visibility plus costly selection, not the shared word “loop.” The outcome link remains a hypothesis until observed use is recorded.

## Falsifiable prediction

During the next two pack reviews, enforcing queue visibility and closed dispositions should leave no over-cap unresolved items and should produce at least one recorded merge, demotion, rejection, or downstream decision. Falsify or demote the pattern if equivalent workflows without visible review perform as well on unresolved count and decision change, or if review only adds ceremony.

## Strongest objection and rival explanation

The documents may share design ancestry, and lower accumulation may result from simply generating less rather than from selection. Review can also optimize for visible outputs and discard slow-burn value.

## Strategic-interaction and Goodhart check

Triggered because contributors and reviewers can adapt to the visible “decision delta” proxy. Contributors are incentivized to produce easy-to-count changes; reviewers can prefer legible short-term artifacts. Under repetition, a plausible equilibrium is many low-cost changes that pass the proxy without improving decisions. Countermeasure: inspect source-of-truth outcomes, count merges/demotions as valid work, preserve slow-burn deferrals, and falsify the mechanism when review changes no retention. No adversary is assumed.

## Creative-to-empirical firewall

Neither input is creative evidence, but both are design prescriptions rather than empirical outcome studies. The pattern therefore remains a pack-design hypothesis. Promotion to an empirical claim requires observed target-domain queue outcomes and a comparison capable of exposing review ceremony.

## Constructive counterweight

Unfiltered generation can support exploration and preserve weak early signals. The constructive alternative is not zero generation: retain immutable Raw evidence, allow bounded speculative candidates, and use dated `watch` return conditions so slow-burn value is not discarded merely because it lacks immediate use.

## Non-applicability

Do not transfer this pattern to immutable compliance retention, raw evidence preservation, emergency alerts that must all be delivered, or low-volume work where a queue costs more than it resolves. If no selective retention decision exists, route the analogy as `non_connection`.
