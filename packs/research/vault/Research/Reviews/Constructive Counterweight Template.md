# Constructive Counterweight — PATTERN OR CLAIM

## Critique under review

Name the pathology, failure, or objection being tested.

## Strongest constructive alternative

Describe a mechanism that could produce coordination, repair, learning, resilience, or legitimate benefit under stated conditions.

## Evidence and boundary

Name evidence for the counterweight, its failure conditions, and the population or time scale where it applies.

## If no constructive counterweight exists

State why none is supported after search; do not invent symmetry. Name the search performed and evidence that could reopen the result.

## Decision effect

Record whether the counterweight narrows, balances, leaves unchanged, or defeats the original claim.
