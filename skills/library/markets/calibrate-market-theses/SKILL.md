# Calibrate Market Theses

## Declare mode and authority

Use research-only or paper mode. State the question, universe, horizon, as-of date, resolver, and source of record. Never place trades, access brokers or wallets, claim fills, or provide personalized execution instructions.

## Build the thesis ladder

Require a grounded source spine, explicit causal mechanism, relevant asset or entity universe, material exposure, valuation or market-structure check, catalyst and timing, liquidity and risk, falsifier, and review date. Separate price indexes from total-return series and contemporaneous data from later revisions.

## Calibrate rather than narrate

Record a probability or confidence range, base rate, strongest objection, rival explanation, and what outcome would resolve the claim. Treat historical analogies as hypotheses, not forecasts. Check whether the apparent edge survives known information, costs, regime changes, and a no-action alternative.

At resolution, append the outcome, compare it with the forecast as originally stated, explain error sources, and update a rule only when the evidence supports a reusable change. Do not rewrite the original prediction.

## Return a research disposition

Show source quality, mechanism, missing data, falsifier, review date, and supported next state. `watch`, `no-action`, or `needs-human` are legitimate outcomes; absence of a thesis is better than forced conviction.
