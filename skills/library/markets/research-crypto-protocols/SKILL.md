# Research Crypto Protocols

## Resolve identity first

Verify chain, contract address, token role, official documentation, and as-of timestamp before using market or protocol data. Symbols and project names are not unique identifiers. Keep research-only authority: no wallet actions, signing, swaps, bridging, staking, or live execution.

## Analyze economic structure

Record price, circulating market capitalization, fully diluted valuation, circulating and maximum supply, emissions, unlock schedule, insider or treasury concentration, and token utility. Distinguish token value capture from protocol activity.

## Analyze market structure and fundamentals

Check venue concentration, usable depth, slippage, borrow availability where relevant, and whether reported volume is credible. Review TVL, fees, revenue, active users, retention, incentives, and dependency on subsidized activity with definitions and source dates. Market capitalization is not exit liquidity.

## Analyze technical and governance risk

Map audits, exploit history, upgradeability, admin keys, oracle and bridge dependencies, validator or sequencer concentration, governance powers, pause controls, and incident response. An audit is evidence about reviewed scope, not a guarantee.

## Stress the thesis

Use explicit scenario assumptions for supply, price, revenue, adoption, and liquidity. State catalysts, strongest objections, falsifiers, missing data, and non-applicability. End with a research disposition and the next source-of-record check; never imply execution occurred.
