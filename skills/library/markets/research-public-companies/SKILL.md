# Research Public Companies

## Bound the research

State the company identifier, question, as-of date, comparison set, and research-only authority. Do not place orders, claim fills, or convert general research into personalized financial advice.

## Build the evidence spine

Prioritize regulatory filings and official reports, then earnings calls, investor materials, company releases, reputable independent reporting, and finally social sources as leads. Preserve reporting period, units, definitions, and source handles.

## Test the thesis

Assess:

- business model and value drivers;
- segment and geographic materiality;
- revenue quality, margins, cash flow, balance sheet, dilution, and accounting caveats;
- valuation under explicit assumptions and relevant peers;
- catalysts with timing and dependency;
- competitive, regulatory, operational, and financing risks;
- expectations already reflected in price; and
- falsifiers and a review date.

Thematic exposure is not thesis materiality. Separate direct economic exposure from marketing language and second-order narratives.

## Report uncertainty

Label verified facts, estimates, and unknowns. Show missing data and sensitivity rather than hiding it in a point estimate. End with a research disposition such as watch, no-action, or needs-human, plus the next evidence that could change it.
